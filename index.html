The asymmetry

The other side of the table has all the data.

When your contract comes up for renewal, the insurer knows precisely where your rates sit relative to every comparable group in the market. Historically, you did not. That information gap is the whole negotiation.

The same procedure pays markedly more with one insurer than another — and with a single insurer, two practices in the same city can sit at opposite ends of a range that routinely spans 2×. If you have never benchmarked against the disclosures, you cannot know which end you are on.
